      SkoolLive: Monetizing Education

      How did this nationwide scam invade the district?

      SkoolLive is a third party company that is monetizing children and education throughout Chandler Unified School District by putting unfiltered kiosks in the school that endanger student privacy, display vulgar messages, and subject children to advertisements. And all the while, CUSD potentially loses money. The back-ended nature of how the kiosks were implemented, has caused the nature of SkoolLive to be unknown by the majority of parties. Parents, students, and school faculty all deserve to have a complete understanding of what students are being subjected to.

      In an effort to learn about why and how the halls had been converted to ad space, research on SkoolLive was initiated with questions to the appropriate officials regarding its origins in the school. It was soon realized that nobody in the school seemed to be able to identify how exactly the kiosks got here.

      Administration directed the search to the school's student government, who circled investigations back to the district. After many pointing fingers, the contract between SkoolLive and CUSD revealed the signator, Frank Fletcher. Further examination of the contract led to multiple cases of SkoolLiveâs considerable violation of district policies, as well as state laws. The school district has responded negatively to the investigation through various forms of denial, threats, and disciplinary actions.

      It is pertinent that public knowledge of SkoolLive and their actions be stressed and expanded. It is not only the right of each student to know about what is going on in their school, but also that of every faculty member of every school, every parent, and every student.

      SkoolLive is a company that scams both students and schools across the nation, and has caught the attention of several advocates for education and students rights. It is our duty to hold public schools accountable for the safety of their children and for this, they must be informed of what is going on within the walls of not only high schools within the Chandler Unified School District, but the country as well.
